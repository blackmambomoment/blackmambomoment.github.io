What is Guaranteed Car Finance for Bad Credit?#

Guaranteed car finance is a special type of car financing that makes getting a car loan easier for people with bad credit or no credit history. How does it work? Unlike traditional car loans, guaranteed car finance involves introducing a guarantor who agrees to take responsibility for the debt if you can’t repay it.

Eligibility for Guaranteed Car Finance with Bad Credit#

Can everyone with bad credit get guaranteed car finance? Not really. Lenders have criteria in place. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • A UK resident with a valid driving license
  • Minimum income threshold
  • Proof of address
  • Employment details
  • Details of your guarantor

Types of Guaranteed Car Finance Available#

You have options even with bad credit:

  • Guarantor Loans: Involves a guarantor co-signing the loan agreement, taking responsibility for the debt if you default.
  • Hire Purchase (HP): You pay a fixed monthly amount towards the car’s value, and once all payments are complete, you own the car.
  • Personal Contract Purchase (PCP): Similar to HP, with an option to pay a final “balloon payment” at the end to own the car or return it.

Step-by-Step Guide to Applying for Guaranteed Car Finance with Bad Credit#

Are you looking to buy a car but have been turned down for a loan because of your bad credit? Don’t worry, you’re not alone. Many people in the UK find themselves in the same situation. But did you know that there are lenders who offer guaranteed car finance even to those with poor credit?

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the step-by-step process of applying for guaranteed car finance with bad credit. We’ll cover everything you need to know, from understanding the different types of finance available to choosing the right lender and completing the application.

Key Takeaways:#

  • Guaranteed car finance is a viable option for people with bad credit who need a car.
  • There are different types of guaranteed car finance available, each with its own eligibility criteria and terms.
  • Lenders will typically require a guarantor or a larger deposit for guaranteed car finance.
  • You should always compare interest rates and fees from multiple lenders before applying.
  • It’s important to be truthful and accurate when completing the application.

Step 1: Research Your Options#

The first step is to research your options and understand the different types of guaranteed car finance available. The most common types are:

  • Guarantor loans: These loans require you to have a guarantor who is willing to co-sign the loan and take on the responsibility of repaying the debt if you default.
  • Hire Purchase (HP): With HP, you pay a deposit and then make monthly payments to the lender. Once you’ve made all the payments, you own the car.
  • Personal Contract Purchase (PCP): With PCP, you make monthly payments to the lender and at the end of the agreement, you have the option to purchase the car outright, return it to the lender, or trade it in for a new car.

Step 2: Choose a Lender#

Once you’ve decided which type of finance is right for you, it’s time to choose a lender. There are a number of lenders who offer guaranteed car finance to people with bad credit. It’s important to compare interest rates and fees from multiple lenders before making a decision.

Some of the lenders that offer guaranteed car finance to people with bad credit include:

  • Car Finance 247
  • Bad Credit Car Finance
  • MoneyJar
  • Auto Approve
  • CarShop

Step 3: Gather Your Documents#

Before you can apply for guaranteed car finance, you’ll need to gather some documents. These typically include:

  • Your ID (passport or driving license)
  • Proof of address (utility bill or bank statement)
  • Proof of income (payslips or bank statements)
  • Details of your guarantor (if required)
  • Details of the car you want to buy

Step 4: Complete the Application#

Once you have all your documents, you can complete the application. The application process will vary depending on the lender. However, you’ll typically need to provide information about yourself, your financial situation, and the car you want to buy.

Step 5: Get a Decision#

Once you’ve submitted your application, the lender will review it and make a decision. This can take a few days or even weeks. If your application is approved, you’ll be sent a loan agreement to sign. Once you’ve signed the agreement, you can collect your car.

Step 6: Repay Your Loan#

Once you’ve collected your car, you need to start repaying your loan. Make sure you make your payments on time and in full each month. If you miss a payment, you could damage your credit score and make it more difficult to get credit in the future.

Enhancing Your Application#

  • Providing a larger deposit can increase your chances of approval and may help you get a lower interest rate.

  • Buying a less expensive car can make it easier to get approved and may result in lower monthly payments.

FAQ#

Q1: Is guaranteed car finance for bad credit in the UK really guaranteed?#

A1: No, there is no such thing as 100% guaranteed car finance. However, there are lenders who specialise in providing loans to people with bad credit, and they may be more likely to approve your application than a traditional lender.

Q2: What are the interest rates on guaranteed car finance for bad credit?#

A2: Interest rates on guaranteed car finance for bad credit are typically higher than those on loans for people with good credit. This is because lenders are taking on more risk when they lend to someone with a bad credit history.

Q3: What are the eligibility requirements for guaranteed car finance for bad credit?#

A3: Eligibility requirements for guaranteed car finance for bad credit vary from lender to lender. However, most lenders will require you to have a regular income, a valid driving license, and a deposit.

Q4: What are the benefits of guaranteed car finance for bad credit?#

A4: The benefits of guaranteed car finance for bad credit include the ability to get a car without having to save up for a large deposit, the opportunity to build your credit score over time, and the peace of mind of knowing that you have a reliable and affordable way to get around.

Q5: What are the drawbacks of guaranteed car finance for bad credit?#

A5: The drawbacks of guaranteed car finance for bad credit include the higher interest rates, the potential for additional fees and charges, and the risk of repossession if you default on your loan.
