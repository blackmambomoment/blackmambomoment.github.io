Different types of guarantor loans#

Guarantor loans are designed to make borrowing money more accessible to individuals with poor or thin credit histories. Unlike traditional loans, guarantor loans involve a third party, known as a guarantor, who agrees to repay the loan if the borrower defaults.

How guarantor loans work?#

When you apply for a guarantor loan, you’ll need to find a guarantor who is willing to sign the loan agreement with you. The guarantor must be someone you trust, as they will be legally responsible for repaying the loan if you can’t.

Once you’ve found a guarantor, you can apply for a loan from a lender. The lender will assess your application and decide whether to approve the loan. If your application is approved, you’ll receive the loan amount in your bank account.

You’ll then make monthly payments to the lender until the loan is repaid. If you can’t make the payments, your guarantor will be responsible for repaying the loan.

Benefits of taking out a guarantor loan#

There are a number of benefits to taking out a guarantor loan, including:

  • You can borrow money even if you have a poor or thin credit history.

  • You may be able to get a lower interest rate than you would with a traditional loan.

  • You can build your credit history by making regular payments on your loan.

Risks of taking out a guarantor loan#

There are also some risks associated with taking out a guarantor loan, including:

  • If you default on your loan, your guarantor will be responsible for repaying the loan. This could damage their credit history and make it difficult for them to borrow money in the future.

  • If your guarantor dies or becomes insolvent, you will be responsible for repaying the loan.

  • You may be charged a higher interest rate than you would with a traditional loan.

Where can you find a guarantor loan?#

You can find guarantor loans from a variety of lenders, including banks, credit unions, and online lenders. It’s important to shop around and compare interest rates before choosing a lender.
