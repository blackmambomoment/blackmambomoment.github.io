How Much Do You Get Paid to Host an International Student in New Zealand#

Hosting international students in New Zealand can be a rewarding experience, both culturally and financially. Host families are compensated for their role in providing a safe and supportive home for these students, and the amount they receive can vary depending on several factors.

So, how much do you get paid to host an international student nz?#

The compensation for hosting an international student in New Zealand typically ranges from NZ$250 to NZ$400 per week, depending on the location of the host family, the age and number of students being hosted, and the level of care and support provided.

Factors Affecting Compensation:

  • Location: Host families in larger cities, such as Auckland and Wellington, generally receive higher compensation than those in smaller towns or rural areas.

  • Age and Number of Students: Families hosting younger students or multiple students may receive additional compensation.

  • Level of Care and Support: Providing extra services, such as transportation or tutoring, may increase the compensation.

Benefits of Hosting International Students:#

  1. Cultural Exchange: Welcoming students from different cultures into your home offers a unique opportunity for both the host family and the student to learn about different perspectives and expand their cultural horizons.

  2. Financial Rewards: Hosting international students can provide a stable source of income for host families, helping to offset the costs of living in New Zealand.

  3. Building Lifelong Relationships: Many host families develop strong bonds with the students they host, often maintaining these relationships long after the students return home.

How to Become a Host Family:#

  1. Research: Gather information about the requirements and expectations of hosting international students in New Zealand.

  2. Prepare Your Home: Ensure your home is comfortable, safe, and has adequate space for the student.

  3. Application: Submit an application to an accredited hosting agency, providing personal information, references, and a home inspection report.

  4. Background Check: Undergo a background check to ensure your suitability as a host family.

  5. Training: Participate in training sessions organized by the hosting agency to learn about the needs and expectations of international students.

Hosting international students can be a fulfilling experience, offering both cultural enrichment and financial benefits. By providing a supportive and welcoming home, host families play a vital role in the success of international students in New Zealand.

FAQ#

Q1: Is there a set amount that I get paid for hosting an international student in New Zealand?#

A1: There is no set amount that you get paid for hosting an international student in New Zealand. The amount you receive varies depending on the organization you are hosting through, the age and needs of the student, and the location of your home. Generally, you can expect to receive anywhere from NZ$200 to NZ$400 per week for hosting a student.

Q2: What expenses are covered by the homestay payment?#

A2: The homestay payment typically covers the student’s accommodation, meals, and utilities. In some cases, it may also cover the cost of transportation to and from school. However, it is important to discuss with the organization you are hosting through what expenses will be covered before you agree to host a student.

Q3: Are there any additional benefits to hosting an international student?#

A3: In addition to the financial benefits, there are many other benefits to hosting an international student. These include the opportunity to learn about a new culture, make new friends, and expand your worldview. Hosting a student can also be a rewarding experience for your family and can help you to build lasting relationships.

Q4: What kind of support is available to homestay families?#

A4: There is a range of support available to homestay families in New Zealand. This includes orientation and training, 24/7 support, and access to resources and information. Many organizations also offer support groups and social events for homestay families.

Q5: How can I find out more about hosting an international student?#

A5: There are a number of ways to find out more about hosting an international student in New Zealand. You can contact an organization that specializes in student homestay, attend an information session, or talk to other homestay families. You can also find more information online.
