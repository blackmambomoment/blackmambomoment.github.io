Navigating NSFAS Funding Amounts#

NSFAS funding for UNISA students is based on several factors, including your household income, academic performance, and course of study. The amount you receive may vary depending on these criteria:

  • Household Income: NSFAS considers the combined annual income of your household to determine your eligibility and funding amount. The lower your household income, the more financial aid you’re likely to receive.

  • Academic Performance: Your academic record plays a role in determining your NSFAS funding. Students with good academic standing are more likely to receive higher funding amounts.

  • Course of Study: The course you’re enrolled in can also impact your NSFAS funding. Certain courses, such as those in the health sciences or engineering, may receive higher funding allocations.

How Much Can You Expect to Receive?#

The amount of NSFAS funding you receive per month will depend on the factors mentioned above. However, as a general guideline, undergraduate students at UNISA can expect to receive around R1,700 to R2,500 per month during the academic year. Postgraduate students may receive higher funding amounts, typically ranging from R2,600 to R3,500 per month.

Additional Financial Support#

In addition to NSFAS funding, UNISA offers several other financial aid options to assist students in covering their education costs. These include:

  • Bursaries: UNISA offers a variety of bursaries to deserving students based on academic merit, financial need, or specific criteria set by the donor.

  • Scholarships: UNISA also offers scholarships to outstanding students who demonstrate exceptional academic achievement or talent in specific areas.

  • Student Loans: Students may apply for student loans from commercial banks or government-backed loan programs to help cover their education expenses.

How Much Does NSFAS Give Students Per Month?#

NSFAS funding covers the following expenses:

  • Tuition fees
  • Prescribed course material allowances (e.g., textbooks and stationery)
  • Living allowances

The living allowance is paid directly to the student’s bank account every month and is intended to cover living expenses such as accommodation, food, and transportation.

The amount of the living allowance varies depending on several factors, including:

  • The type of qualification the student is pursuing
  • The university or college the student is attending
  • The student’s financial need

The base amount of NSFAS funding is determined by the Department of Higher Education and Training (DHET) and is adjusted annually. For the 2023 academic year, the base amount for living allowances is:

  • R15,750 for students living in private accommodation
  • R1,600 for students living in NSFAS-accredited accommodation

In addition to the base amount, NSFAS may also provide additional funding to students who have financial need. This additional funding is known as a “top-up” and is determined on a case-by-case basis.

NSFAS has a handy tool to estimate the living allowance amount you can expect if you are eligible for NSFAS funding, you can use their online calculator.

Ultimately, NSFAS is designed to help students from disadvantaged backgrounds afford a higher education. If you’re eligible, NSFAS funding can be a valuable source of financial support.

FAQ#

Q1: How much funding does NSFAS provide to students at UNISA per month?#

A1: The amount of funding that NSFAS provides to students at UNISA per month varies depending on the student’s financial need and academic program. NSFAS funding typically covers tuition fees, accommodation costs, and a living allowance. The living allowance is meant to cover basic living expenses such as food, transportation, and books.

Q2: What are the eligibility criteria for NSFAS funding at UNISA?#

A2: To be eligible for NSFAS funding at UNISA, students must meet the following criteria:

  • Be a South African citizen or permanent resident.
  • Have a household income of R350,000 or less per year.
  • Be registered for a qualifying program at UNISA.
  • Meet the academic requirements set by NSFAS.

Q3: How do I apply for NSFAS funding at UNISA?#

A3: To apply for NSFAS funding at UNISA, students must complete an online application form. The application form can be accessed on the NSFAS website. Students will need to provide information about their financial status, academic record, and personal circumstances.

Q4: When are NSFAS applications due?#

A4: NSFAS applications for the upcoming academic year typically open in August or September. The deadline for applications is usually in December or January. Students are encouraged to apply early to avoid missing out on funding.

Q5: How can I find out if I have been approved for NSFAS funding?#

A5: Students can check their NSFAS application status online. The NSFAS website will provide updates on the status of the application. Students will also be notified via email or SMS if their application has been approved or denied.
