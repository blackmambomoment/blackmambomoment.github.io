How Much Does NSFAS Give Students Per Month in 2021: A Comprehensive Explanation

The National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) is a South African government program that provides financial assistance to students from poor and working-class families. The amount of NSFAS funding that a student receives depends on a number of factors, including their household income, academic results, and the cost of their studies. In this article, we will provide a comprehensive explanation of how much NSFAS gives students per month in 2021.

How Much Does NSFAS Give Students Per Month in 2021?

NSFAS Funding Allocations

NSFAS funding for 2021 is allocated as follows:

  • Tuition fees: 100% coverage of tuition fees up to the institution’s approved fee.

  • Accommodation: R15,000 per year for students living off-campus and R4,500 per year for day scholars.

  • Books and learning materials: R8,000 per year.

  • Transport: R10,000 per year.

  • Personal care: R5,000 per year.

  • Incidental expenses: R2,000 per year.

Disbursement Schedule

NSFAS funding is disbursed on a monthly basis, in line with the following schedule:

  • January: Tuition fees, accommodation, and books and learning materials.

  • February: Transport and personal care.

  • March to November: Transport, personal care, and incidental expenses.

  • December: Final installment of accommodation.

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for NSFAS funding, students must meet the following criteria:

  • Be a South African citizen or permanent resident.

  • Be registered for a full-time undergraduate degree or diploma at a public university or TVET college.

  • Meet the NSFAS income threshold.

  • Not have already obtained a qualification at the same level or higher.

  • Not be in default on any NSFAS loan.

Application Process

  3. Submit your application.

Eligibility criteria for NSFAS funding:#

In this section, we will be discussing the eligibility criteria for National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) funding. The application procedure and funding allocation process will be thoroughly explained.

Key Takeaways:

  • NSFAS is a financial aid scheme in South Africa that provides funding to students from poor and working-class families to study at public universities and Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) colleges.

  • To be eligible for NSFAS funding, a student must:

  • Be a South African citizen or permanent resident.

  • Be registered at a public university or TVET college in South Africa.

  • Come from a household with a combined annual income of R350,000 or less.

  • Be enrolled for a first degree, diploma, or certificate qualification.

  • Have a valid ID document.

  • Not be in receipt of any other financial aid or bursary.

  • The amount of NSFAS funding that a student receives depends on their household income, academic performance, and the cost of their studies.

  • Students from households with an annual income of R0 to R350,000 receive full funding, which covers the cost of tuition, accommodation, meals, and books.

  • Students from households with an annual income of R350,001 to R600,000 receive partial funding, which covers the cost of tuition and accommodation.

  • Students from households with an annual income of R600,001 or more are not eligible for NSFAS funding.

  • NSFAS funding is disbursed on a monthly basis.

  • The amount of funding that a student receives each month depends on their household income and the cost of their studies.

  • Students are not required to repay NSFAS funding.

FAQ#

Q1: How much funding does NSFAS provide to students per month in 2021?#

A1: The amount of funding that NSFAS provides to students per month in 2021 depends on various factors, including the student’s level of study, institutional type, and study mode. Generally, the funding ranges from R1,290 to R1,920 for TVET colleges and R1,650 to R2,430 for higher education institutions.

Q2: Are there any additional allowances or subsidies that NSFAS students receive?#

A2: Yes, NSFAS provides additional allowances and subsidies to students, such as a living allowance, book allowance, transport allowance, and a once-off laptop allowance for students in their first year of study. The amounts of these allowances vary depending on the student’s level of study and institutional type.

Q3: When are NSFAS funds disbursed to students?#

A3: NSFAS funds are typically disbursed to students in two installments per semester. The first installment is paid at the beginning of the semester, and the second installment is paid in the middle of the semester. The exact dates of disbursement vary depending on the institution.

Q4: What are the eligibility criteria for receiving NSFAS funding?#

A4: To be eligible for NSFAS funding, students must be South African citizens or permanent residents, must be registered for a qualification at a public university or TVET college, must meet the financial means test, and must not be in default on any previous NSFAS loan.

Q5: How can students apply for NSFAS funding?#

A5: Students can apply for NSFAS funding online through the myNSFAS portal. The application process typically opens in September each year, and students must submit their applications before the closing date.
