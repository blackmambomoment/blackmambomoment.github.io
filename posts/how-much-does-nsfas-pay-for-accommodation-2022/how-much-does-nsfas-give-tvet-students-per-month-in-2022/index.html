How Much Does NSFAS Give TVET Students per Month in 2022?#

The National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) provides funding to students in South Africa who are enrolled in public Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) colleges. NSFAS funding covers a variety of expenses, including tuition fees, accommodation, and living expenses.

How much funding do TVET students receive?

The amount of funding that TVET students receive each month depends on their financial need and the type of program they are enrolled in.

  • Tuition fees: NSFAS covers tuition fees up to a maximum of R122,000 per year.

  • Accommodation: TVET students who live in NSFAS-accredited accommodation receive a monthly allowance of R4,200.

  • Living expenses: TVET students receive a monthly living allowance of R1,875.

Additional benefits

In addition to tuition fees, accommodation, and living expenses, NSFAS also provides a number of additional benefits to TVET students, including:

  • Free textbooks: TVET students receive a free set of textbooks at the beginning of each academic year.

  • Transport allowance: TVET students who live more than 10 kilometers from their TVET college receive a monthly transport allowance of R450.

  • Medical aid: TVET students are eligible for free medical aid through the National Health Insurance (NHI) scheme.

How to apply for NSFAS funding

To apply for NSFAS funding, students must meet the following requirements:

  • Must be a South African citizen or permanent resident
  • Must be registered at a public TVET college
  • Must have a combined household income of less than R350,000 per year
  • Must not have previously graduated from a TVET college or university

Students can apply for NSFAS funding online or at their local TVET college. The application process typically takes 4-6 weeks.

Application Process for NSFAS Funding#

Navigating the Application Process for NSFAS Funding as a TVET student can be challenging, so here are a few essential steps to guide you through.

  1. Gather the Necessary Documents:

    • Certified copy of your ID or unabridged birth certificate
    • Proof of income for your household
    • Academic records from your previous institution
    • A signed declaration form from your TVET institution confirming your registration

  2. Complete the NSFAS Application Form Online:

    • Visit the NSFAS website and create an account
    • Provide personal information, contact details, and academic details
    • Upload the required documents
    • Submit the application and wait for the outcome

  3. Attend a Verification Appointment:

    • Once your application is processed, you will be invited for a verification appointment
    • Bring the original copies of your documents for verification
    • Provide any additional information or documents requested by NSFAS

  4. Monitor Your Application Status:

    • You can track the progress of your application online
    • Check for updates and notifications regularly
    • Contact NSFAS if you have any queries or concerns

  5. Receive Your Funding:

    • If your application is successful, you will be notified of the funding amount approved
    • The funding will be paid directly to your TVET institution or to a designated bank account

Q1: How much funding does NSFAS provide to TVET students per month in 2022?#

A1: In 2022, NSFAS offers financial assistance to TVET students ranging from ZAR 28,644 to ZAR 42,972 per academic year. The actual amount disbursed per month depends on the student’s course of study and their financial needs, as determined by the NSFAS assessment process.

Q2: What are the eligibility criteria for TVET students to receive NSFAS funding?#

A2: To be eligible for NSFAS funding, TVET students must meet specific criteria, including being a South African citizen or permanent resident, having a household income below the threshold set by NSFAS, and being enrolled in a TVET program accredited by the relevant authorities.

Q3: How can TVET students apply for NSFAS funding?#

A3: TVET students can apply for NSFAS funding online through the NSFAS website or by visiting the nearest NSFAS office. The application process typically opens in September of each year, and students are required to submit various documents, including proof of identity, academic records, and financial information.

Q4: What are the different types of NSFAS funding available to TVET students?#

A4: NSFAS provides various types of funding to TVET students, including tuition fees, living allowances, and travel allowances. The living allowance is intended to cover students’ basic living expenses, such as accommodation, meals, and transportation, while the travel allowance is meant to assist students with the cost of traveling to and from their educational institutions.

Q5: When can TVET students expect to receive their NSFAS funding?#

A5: The disbursement of NSFAS funding to TVET students typically occurs in two installments per semester. The first installment is usually paid at the beginning of the academic year, while the second installment is paid in the middle of the semester. Students can expect to receive their funding within a few weeks of their application being approved.
