How Much Does NSFAS Give University Students Per Month#

In this comprehensive breakdown, we’ll uncover the funding details of NSFAS and provide a detailed account of how much NSFAS gives university students per month.

NSFAS Financial Aid Breakdown:

  • Tuition and Registration: NSFAS covers full tuition and registration fees, relieving students from this significant financial burden.
  • Allowance:
    • Monthly living allowance: University students receive a monthly living allowance to cover basic needs such as groceries, rent, and transportation. The exact amount varies depending on the institution and qualification, typically ranging between R1,500 to R3,930 per month.
    • Incidental allowance: A once-off payment is provided to cover expenses related to textbooks and learning materials. This allowance is typically between R550 and R1,200.
    • Travel allowance: Students who live more than 30 kilometers from their institution may qualify for a travel allowance to cover transportation costs.
  • Loans:
    • Loan for laptops or tablets: NSFAS provides loans to assist students with purchasing laptops or tablets to facilitate their studies. The loan amount for laptops ranges from R5,720 to R15,000, and for tablets, it’s between R2,520 and R4,000.
    • Loans for accommodation and meals: Students who live in accredited private residences or off-campus accommodations can apply for loans to cover these expenses.

Eligibility for NSFAS Funding:

  • South African citizenship or permanent residency
  • Registered at a public university or TVET college
  • Meeting the financial criteria as set by NSFAS
  • Pursuing a qualification that is funded by NSFAS

Applying for NSFAS Funding:

  • Application periods typically open in August/September each year
  • Submit a complete application form with all required supporting documents
  • Applications can be submitted online or at a NYDA center

Eligibility Criteria for NSFAS Funding#

College can be an expensive endeavor, with tuition, books, and living expenses quickly adding up. Thankfully, financial assistance is available to help students cover these costs, with the National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) being a crucial resource for South African students. However, not everyone is eligible for NSFAS funding. There are specific criteria that must be met to receive assistance from NSFAS.

Household Income Threshold:#

  • NSFAS considers the combined annual household income of the student’s family as a determining factor for eligibility.
  • In 2023, the threshold was set at R350,000.
  • If the household income exceeds this amount, the student is not eligible to apply for NSFAS funding.

Academic Requirements:#

  • Academic performance also plays a role in NSFAS eligibility.
  • Students must meet certain academic requirements to qualify for funding.
  • These requirements vary depending on the program of study and the institution.
  • Generally, students must achieve a minimum academic average and meet specific subject requirements.

South African Citizenship or Residency:#

  • NSFAS funding is only available to South African citizens or permanent residents.
  • International students are not eligible to apply.

Age Limit:#

  • There is an age restriction for NSFAS eligibility.
  • Students must be between the ages of 18 and 35 to apply for funding.
  • However, certain exceptions may apply, such as students with disabilities or students who were previously disadvantaged.

Disability:#

  • NSFAS recognizes that students with disabilities may face additional financial challenges.
  • Special provisions are available for students with disabilities to apply for funding.
  • These provisions include additional grants and support services.

Criminal Record:#

  • A criminal record can impact NSFAS eligibility.
  • Students with a criminal record may be denied funding.
  • However, certain factors, such as the nature and severity of the offense and the time since it was committed, may be considered in the assessment.

Registration:#

  • Students must be registered at a public university or Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) college to be eligible for NSFAS funding.
  • Only programs registered with the Department of Higher Education and Training are eligible.

Key Takeaways:

  • NSFAS eligibility is determined by various factors, including household income, academic performance, citizenship, age, disability, and criminal record.
  • Applicants must meet the NSFAS requirements and submit a comprehensive application.
  • NSFAS offers financial assistance to eligible students in the form of grants, loans, and bursaries.
  • NSFAS is a valuable resource for students from disadvantaged backgrounds, enabling them to access higher education.
  • NSFAS continuously strives to provide accessible and equitable financial aid to deserving students.

Factors that Determine NSFAS Funding Amounts#

NSFAS allocates funding to students based on a comprehensive assessment of several factors:

  1. Household Income:

    • The most significant factor is the household income of the student’s family.
    • NSFAS considers the combined income of all individuals living in the student’s household.
    • Students from households with lower incomes receive higher funding amounts.

  2. Academic Results:

    • NSFAS rewards academic merit by providing higher funding amounts to students with better academic records.
    • Matric results are typically used to assess academic performance.
    • Students with higher matric results may be eligible for additional funding.

  3. Other Individual Circumstances:

    • NSFAS also considers other factors, such as disability status and family circumstances.
    • Disability grants may be available to students with disabilities.
    • Students facing financial hardship due to family circumstances may receive additional support.

FAQ#

Q1: How much NSFAS funding do university students receive per month?#

A1: The amount of NSFAS funding that university students receive per month depends on several factors, including their family’s income, the type of qualification they are pursuing, and whether they are a first-year or returning student. In general, NSFAS funding for university students ranges from R1,500 to R5,500 per month.

Q2: What are the different types of NSFAS funding available to university students?#

A2: NSFAS offers various types of funding to university students, including:

  • Bursaries: NSFAS bursaries are awarded to students who meet specific academic requirements and demonstrate financial need. Bursaries cover the full cost of tuition and living expenses.
  • Loans: NSFAS loans are available to students who do not qualify for a bursary. Loans must be repaid after the student has completed their studies.
  • Work-study programs: NSFAS work-study programs allow students to work part-time while they are studying. This can help students to earn money to cover their living expenses.

Q3: How do I apply for NSFAS funding?#

A3: To apply for NSFAS funding, students must complete an online application form. The application form is available on the NSFAS website. Students will need to provide information about their family’s income, their academic record, and their chosen course of study.

Q4: When is the NSFAS application deadline?#

A4: The NSFAS application deadline is typically in November each year. However, students are encouraged to apply as early as possible to avoid missing out on funding.

Q5: How can I track the status of my NSFAS application?#

A5: Students can track the status of their NSFAS application by logging into their NSFAS account. They will be able to see if their application has been received, processed, or approved.
