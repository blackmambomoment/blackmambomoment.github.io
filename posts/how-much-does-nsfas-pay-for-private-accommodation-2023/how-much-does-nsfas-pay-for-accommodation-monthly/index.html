FAQ#

Q1: How much does NSFAS pay for accommodation monthly in 2023?#

A1: As of the 2023 academic year, NSFAS provides up to R4,600 per month to cover accommodation costs for students residing in NSFAS-accredited private residences or off-campus housing. NSFAS reviews and adjusts accommodation rates annually, considering economic factors and the rising cost of living.

Q2: Are all students eligible for the NSFAS accommodation allowance?#

A2: Only students meeting specific criteria are eligible for the NSFAS accommodation allowance. These criteria include being a South African citizen or a permanent resident, meeting the NSFAS income threshold, being registered for a financial aid-approved program, and not receiving any other form of government funding for accommodation.

Q3: How do I apply for the NSFAS accommodation allowance?#

A3: To apply for the NSFAS accommodation allowance, students must complete the NSFAS application form and submit all required supporting documents. Additionally, students must submit proof of their accommodation arrangements, such as a lease agreement or a confirmation letter from their university or college.

Q4: Is the NSFAS accommodation allowance paid directly to students?#

A4: NSFAS does not pay the accommodation allowance directly to students. Instead, the allowance is paid directly to the institution or private accommodation provider. Students must make arrangements with their institution or accommodation provider to ensure that the allowance is used to cover their accommodation costs.

Q5: What are the consequences of misusing the NSFAS accommodation allowance?#

A5: Misusing the NSFAS accommodation allowance, such as using it for purposes other than accommodation, can have serious consequences. Students may have to repay the funds, face disciplinary action from their institution, or even be disqualified from receiving NSFAS funding in the future.
